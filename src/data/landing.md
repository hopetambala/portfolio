Serving in the Peace Corps, I found the spark for the transformative power of technology to bridge gaps, empower communities, and solve the kind of problems that actually keep people up at night. It's the main reason why I even became an engineer and [do the work I do today.](/#workexperiences)

Through candlelit nights pouring over paper health records with no electricity, I learned what it feels like to not have the power of the internet at your fingertips. It’s rough.

Through that reality, I found my why. Today, I’m an experienced software engineer with a decade of skin in the game across cross-platform mobile and web. That passion comes from my work at [Puente](https://puente-dr.org/), a nonprofit I co-founded to build products that actually respond to health needs in real-time.
I still believe the Internet and tech at large should be a tool for human happiness, not just another subscription to manage.

And honestly, I just love to create and [capture beautiful moments](/journal). Whether it's for work or for play, I’m constantly reimagining how we can use this digital canvas to build cool 💩.

If you’re reading this, hopefully that’s also something you’re into.
If it is, let’s talk.
